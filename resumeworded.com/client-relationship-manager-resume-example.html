    Client Relationship Manager Resume Sample

    As the name suggests, these relationship managers focus only on strengthening client relationships. In this position, you will research and approach potential clients, secure sales, and strive to maintain the relationship between the client and the company. This position requires a long history in customer-facing positions so ensure that your resume reflects that as this resume does.

    Your interpersonal skills will also be essential for your success, so work on showing that skill and others by highlighting your successes using that skill. Take a look at this successful resume sample.

    Client Relationship Manager Resume Tips

    We spoke with hiring managers from top companies like Deloitte, Accenture, and IBM to gather their best tips for writing a Client Relationship Manager resume. They've seen thousands of resumes throughout their careers and know exactly what makes a resume stand out. Here are their top 6 tips for crafting a resume that will get you hired.

       Quantify your client relationship impact

    The best way to show the impact you've had in previous roles is to quantify it with numbers and metrics. Compare this:

    • Managed a portfolio of enterprise clients and ensured their success

    To this:

    • Managed a portfolio of 50+ enterprise clients, growing revenue by 25% and improving retention to 95%

    By including numbers, you give hiring managers a clear picture of the scope and results of your work. Aim to include at least 2-3 metrics for each role like revenue generated, client retention rates, account growth rates, or size of client portfolio.


    Expand

       Highlight your industry expertise

    Companies want to hire Client Relationship Managers who deeply understand their industry and can 'speak the language' of their clients. Be sure to highlight your industry expertise throughout your resume, like this:

    • 5+ years of experience managing client relationships in the financial services industry
    • Deep expertise in the challenges facing retail banking clients, including customer retention, digital transformation, and regulatory compliance

    If you have experience across multiple industries, consider tailoring your resume to focus on the most relevant industry for each job you apply to.

       Emphasize your communication and relationship-building skills

    At its core, client relationship management is about building trust, rapport, and long-term partnerships with clients. The best resumes emphasize these 'soft skills' in addition to hard skills and experience. You might say:

    • Skilled at building trusted advisor relationships with C-suite clients through active listening, empathy, and a solutions-oriented approach
    • Exceptional written and verbal communicator, able to translate complex technical concepts for non-technical stakeholders

    Include specific examples of how you've used your relationship-building skills to achieve results, like growing accounts, improving client satisfaction, or collaborating across departments.

       Show progression and increasing responsibility

    Hiring managers want to see that you've progressed in your career and taken on increasing levels of responsibility. Make this clear on your resume by:

    • Grouping your experience into roles of increasing seniority (e.g. Account Coordinator > Account Manager > Senior Account Manager)
    • Using phrases like 'promoted to...' or 'selected to lead...' to highlight advancement
    • Showing how your portfolio of clients or revenue generated has grown over time

    You might also use your resume summary to emphasize career progression, like this:

    Client Relationship Manager with 7+ years of experience spanning Account Coordinator to Senior Account Director roles. Proven track record of growing enterprise SaaS client portfolio from $2M to $15M+ in annual revenue and improving retention to 95%+.

       Use action verbs to showcase your contributions

    When describing your accomplishments, start each bullet point with a strong action verb. Compare:

    • Responsible for a portfolio of mid-market clients in the retail industry

    To:

    • Managed and grew a portfolio of 30+ mid-market retail clients, delivering 115% of revenue targets

    The second example uses the action verb 'managed' to emphasize your active role and impact. Other strong action verbs for a Client Relationship Manager include:

    • Developed
    • Partnered
    • Led
    • Collaborated
    • Delivered
    • Presented

       Include relevant certifications and training

    In addition to your work experience and skills, be sure to include any relevant certifications or training that would be valuable in a Client Relationship Manager role. This might include:

    • Salesforce Certified Administrator
    • HubSpot Inbound Sales Certification
    • SPIN Selling Certification
    • Challenger Sales Training
    • Crucial Conversations Training

    List your certifications in a separate 'Certifications' or 'Training' section at the bottom of your resume to ensure they aren't overlooked. You can also mention them in your resume summary, like:

    Salesforce Certified Administrator with 5+ years of experience managing enterprise client relationships in the SaaS industry.

    Writing Your Client Relationship Manager Resume: Section By Section

      Summary

    A resume summary for a Client Relationship Manager role is optional, but it can be an effective way to provide additional context about your experience and qualifications that may not be immediately apparent from the rest of your resume. It's particularly useful if you're making a career change or have a diverse background that doesn't perfectly align with the Client Relationship Manager role you're targeting.

    When writing your summary, focus on highlighting your most relevant skills, experiences, and accomplishments that demonstrate your ability to succeed in the role. Avoid simply repeating information that's already covered in other sections of your resume, and never use an objective statement that focuses on what you want from the job.

    Expand

    1. Tailor your summary to the client relationship manager role

    When crafting your summary for a Client Relationship Manager position, it's crucial to align your skills and experiences with the specific requirements of the role. Here's what to do:

    • Highlight your experience in building and maintaining strong client relationships
    • Mention your ability to understand and address client needs
    • Demonstrate your communication and problem-solving skills

    Avoid generic statements that could apply to any job, like:

    • Hardworking professional with a proven track record of success
    • Experienced in various industries and roles
    Results-driven Client Relationship Manager with 5+ years of experience in fostering long-term client partnerships. Adept at identifying client needs, providing personalized solutions, and ensuring client satisfaction through effective communication and problem-solving. Committed to delivering exceptional service and driving business growth.
      Ensure your summary includes relevant keywords from the job description to improve your chances of passing applicant tracking systems (ATS). Tools like Targeted Resume can help you optimize your resume against a specific job posting.

    2. Focus on your unique value proposition

    Your summary should quickly convey the unique value you bring to the Client Relationship Manager role. Instead of simply listing your skills or experiences, focus on the impact you've made in your previous positions.

    For example, instead of saying:

    • Experienced in managing client accounts and resolving issues

    Try something like:

    • Successfully managed a portfolio of 50+ clients, increasing client retention by 20% through proactive account management and issue resolution

    By quantifying your achievements and showcasing your impact, you'll create a more compelling summary that sets you apart from other candidates.

      Remember, your summary is your chance to make a strong first impression. Keep it concise, impactful, and tailored to the Client Relationship Manager role you're seeking. For an objective assessment of your resume's strengths and areas for improvement, consider using a tool like Score My Resume.

      Experience

    The work experience section is the heart of your resume as a client relationship manager. It's where you show hiring managers how you've applied your skills to drive results for your clients and employers. Use this section to highlight your most relevant and impressive achievements.

    1. Highlight relationship-building accomplishments

    As a client relationship manager, your top priority is building strong, lasting relationships with key clients. Showcase accomplishments that demonstrate your ability to establish trust, provide exceptional service, and grow accounts.

    Instead of just listing responsibilities like this:

    • Managed a portfolio of 50 client accounts
    • Responded to client inquiries and resolved issues

    Quantify your impact with specific examples:

    • Grew revenue from key accounts by 25% year-over-year by implementing a new client success program
    • Maintained a client retention rate of 95%+ over 3 years
    • Turned around at-risk $500K client account, resolving service issues and expanding contract by 15%
      When describing your accomplishments, use strong action verbs like 'strengthened', 'expanded', 'retained' and 'grew' to emphasize your relationship-building skills. Avoid generic verbs like 'managed' or 'handled'.

    2. Showcase client relationship tools and methods

    Modern client relationship managers rely on a variety of tools and methodologies to effectively serve their accounts. Highlight your proficiency with industry-standard CRM platforms, as well as any specialized tools you've used to improve client engagement and team collaboration.

    Examples:

    • Implemented Salesforce CRM to centralize customer data, resulting in 20% faster response times
    • Utilized Gainsight for customer health scoring, identifying and acting on early warning signs to save at-risk accounts
    • Trained and mentored a team of 5 junior account managers on Miller Heiman Strategic Selling methodology
      Stumped on which skills and tools to include? Run your resume through Score My Resume to check how well your resume showcases the top client relationship manager keywords that employers look for.

    3. Demonstrate cross-functional leadership

    Effective client relationship managers don't operate in a silo. Highlight experiences collaborating with other departments like sales, product, customer service, and upper management to meet client needs and resolve escalations.

    Show how you've led cross-functional initiatives:

    • Collaborated with product team to develop new feature based on feedback from top clients, resulting in 10% usage increase
    • Partnered with customer service to overhaul support processes, cutting resolution times by 25%
    • Prepared and delivered executive-level presentations on client health and revenue growth
      To further showcase your leadership abilities, include examples of how you've mentored and trained junior team members. Bonus points if you can highlight internal promotions you earned.

      Education

    Your education section is a key part of your client relationship manager resume. It shows hiring managers your academic background and relevant coursework. Keep this section concise and highlight your most impressive and relevant educational achievements.


    Expand

    1. Put education at the top if you're a recent grad

    If you graduated within the past few years, consider putting your education section above your work experience. This is especially important if your degree is highly relevant to a client relationship manager role.

    For example:

    Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA), Marketing
    University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI
    Graduation: May 2022
    GPA: 3.8

    Relevant Coursework:

    • Customer Relationship Management
    • Digital Marketing Strategy
    • Business Communications
      If you had a strong GPA (3.5 or above), consider including it to further emphasize your academic achievements.

    2. Keep the education section brief if you're experienced

    If you have several years of client relationship management experience, your education section should be concise. Hiring managers will be more interested in your professional accomplishments.

    Here's an example of what to avoid:

    Master of Business Administration (MBA)
    Harvard University, Cambridge, MA
    Graduation: June 2010
    GPA: 3.9
    Relevant Coursework: Marketing Strategy, Business Analytics, Organizational Behavior, Financial Accounting, Operations Management

    Bachelor of Science in Marketing
    Boston University, Boston, MA
    Graduation: May 2008
    GPA: 3.7
    Relevant Coursework: Consumer Behavior, Market Research, Brand Management, Digital Marketing, Sales Management

    Instead, keep it short and sweet:

    MBA, Harvard University
    BS, Marketing, Boston University

      If you have an advanced degree like an MBA, you can leave off your undergraduate details to keep the section concise.

    3. Include relevant certifications in the education section

    If you have earned certifications that are relevant to a client relationship manager role, consider including them in your education section. This can help demonstrate your expertise and commitment to professional development.

    Some certifications to consider:

    • Certified Sales Professional (CSP)
    • Certified Customer Service Professional (CCSP)
    • Hubspot Inbound Sales Certification
    • Salesforce Certified Administrator

    Here's an example of how to list a certification:

    Hubspot Inbound Sales Certification, 2021

      Only include certifications that are truly relevant and from reputable organizations. Avoid listing generic or outdated certifications.

      Skills

    The skills section of your resume for a Client Relationship Manager role is crucial. It's where you highlight your abilities that are most relevant to the job. Hiring managers and applicant tracking systems (ATS) will scan this section to determine if you have the right mix of hard and soft skills. Here are some tips to make your skills section stand out.


    Expand

    1. Tailor your skills to the client relationship manager job description

    Read the job description carefully and identify the key skills they are looking for in a candidate. Make sure to include these in your skills section, using the same terminology where possible. This will help your resume pass through ATS filters and catch the attention of the hiring manager.

    For example, if the job description mentions:

    • Proficiency in Salesforce CRM
    • Excellent communication skills
    • Ability to manage multiple client accounts

    Make sure to include these skills in your resume, like this:

    • Salesforce CRM (Advanced)
    • Client Communication
    • Account Management
      

    Not sure if your resume has the right skills to pass ATS? Try Resume Worded's Targeted Resume tool. It analyzes your resume against a job description and provides feedback on which key skills you may be missing.

    2. Categorize your skills into relevant groupings

    Grouping your skills into categories makes it easier for hiring managers to quickly see your key competencies. For a client relationship manager role, consider categories such as:

    • CRM Software: Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho CRM
    • Communication: Client Presentations, Relationship Building, Conflict Resolution
    • Account Management: Upselling, Contract Negotiation, Churn Reduction

    Avoid having a long list of unrelated skills, like this:

    • Salesforce
    • Excel
    • Public Speaking
    • Photoshop
    • Account Management

    While you may have all these skills, not grouping related ones together makes your skills section seem unfocused and scattered.

      

    When categorizing your skills, put the most important categories first. For client relationship roles, lead with skills around relationship building, communication, and account management.

    3. Specify your proficiency level for technical skills

    For skills that can be measured, like proficiency in a software or language, specify your level of expertise. This gives hiring managers a clearer picture of your abilities. Use terms like:

    • Basic
    • Intermediate
    • Advanced
    • Expert

    For example:

    • Salesforce CRM (Advanced)
    • HubSpot (Intermediate)
    • Zoho CRM (Basic)

    Avoid vague descriptors or overused terms like this:

    • Salesforce CRM (Highly skilled)
    • HubSpot (Proficient)
    • Zoho CRM (Familiar)

    Using clear proficiency levels gives a more honest representation of your skills and helps you stand out.

      

    Tip: If you're not sure what proficiency level to use, think about how comfortable you would be completing tasks or answering questions about the skill in a job interview. Could you demonstrate advanced functions or speak about it at length? Or do you only have a basic understanding?

    4. Leave out outdated or irrelevant skills

    Hiring managers want to see skills that are current and relevant to the client relationship manager role. Leave out skills that are outdated or not directly applicable, such as:

    • Lotus Notes
    • Fax Machines
    • Rolodexes
    • Cold Calling

    Instead, focus on modern skills and technologies used in client relationship management today, like:

    • Salesforce Einstein Analytics
    • Customer Success Platforms
    • Account Based Marketing
    • Social Selling

    By including current, relevant skills, you show that you're up-to-date with industry trends and have the necessary abilities for the role.
